Polymer and modern web APIs: In production at Google scale

By Matt McNulty & Taylor Savage & Eric Bidelman & Addy Osmani

Google I/O

May 28th, 2015

The latest version of Polymer is fast and lean. Learn how teams at Google have successfully launched sites using Polymer and the latest platform APIs: Web Animations, Service Workers for offline and push notifications, and material design. And if you're new to Polymer we'll show you how to get started building mobile-first apps.

Video

Matt McNulty

@mattsmcnulty

Matthew leads the Polymer team, which is part of the Chrome team at Google. Prior to joining Google, he was responsible for the Enyo framework, Ares development environment, and SDK at webOS.

Taylor Savage

@taylorthesavage

Taylor is the Product Manager of the Polymer team at Google.

Eric Bidelman

@ebidel

Eric is a Staff Developer Programs Engineer on the Google Chrome team. His current passion is web components and Polymer. Eric is the author of 'Using the HTML5 Filesystem API' and regularly contributes to html5rocks.com and other open source projects. Prior to Google, Eric worked as a software engineer at the University of Michigan where he designed rich web applications and APIs for the university’s 19 libraries.

Addy Osmani

@addyosmani

Addy works on the Chrome Developer Relations team at Google, building and advocating for tools to help improve developer productivity and satisfaction. His personal projects include TodoMVC, which helps developers compare JavaScript MVC frameworks and AuraJS. He's also written 'Developing Backbone.js Applications' and 'Learning JavaScript Design Patterns'.
